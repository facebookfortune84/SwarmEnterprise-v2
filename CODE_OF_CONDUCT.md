# Code of Conduct — SwarmEnterprise v2

This Code of Conduct governs participation in the SwarmEnterprise v2 project
(version 2.0.0) and all associated spaces, including GitHub Issues, Pull
Requests, Discussions, the project mailing list, and any official community
events.

---

## Project Pledge

As contributors, maintainers, and leaders, we pledge to make participation in
this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Standards

### Expected Behaviors

The following behaviors are expected of all community members:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other participants
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and
  learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community and the project, not
  solely for individual benefit
- Using inclusive, professional language in all written and verbal
  communications

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable in all project spaces:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and any form of sexual attention
  or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information — such as physical or email addresses
  — without explicit permission
- Threatening or intimidating language, whether in technical disagreements or
  personal interactions
- Sustained disruption of community discussions, code reviews, or events
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards
of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action
in response to any behavior deemed harmful, offensive, threatening, or
otherwise unacceptable.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that
do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for
moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, and also applies when an
individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples
include using an official project email address, posting via an official social
media account, or acting as an appointed representative at a live or online
event.

---

## Reporting Procedure

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainers at:

**ops@realms2riches.com**

Subject line format: `[CODE OF CONDUCT] SwarmEnterprise v2 — <brief description>`

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All
maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.
Reports are acknowledged within **48 hours** on business days.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines in determining consequences for any
violation of this Code of Conduct.

### 1 — Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other unprofessional
behavior.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from maintainers explaining the
nature of the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public
apology may be requested.

### 2 — Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of
related actions.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external
channels such as social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary
or permanent ban.

### 3 — Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication
with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction
with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those
enforcing the Code of Conduct, is permitted during this period. Violating
these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4 — Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violations, harassment of an
individual, or sustained aggression toward, or disparagement of, classes of
individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any public interaction within the
project community.
